Further reading

A short, curated set of links for going deeper: the City's live air-quality map for current conditions, the health case for caring about traffic-related air pollution, the open dataset this site is built on, and the source code for the site itself. More may be added over time.

Live air quality

  • Montréal real-time air-quality map — The City of Montréal's official map of current air-quality conditions across the RSQA network. Where this site shows the historical record, that map shows what currently active stations are reading right now — the latest hourly readings and Air Quality Index (IQA) at each station. Bilingual (FR/EN).

Health

  • Health impacts of traffic-related air pollution in Canada — Health Canada's assessment of how pollution from road traffic affects health. It estimates that roughly four in ten Canadians live within 250 m of a high-traffic road and attributes about 1,200 premature deaths a year to traffic-related air pollution, the mixture of fine particulates and gases (including NO and NO₂) that this site maps. The linked section discusses near-road exposure specifically.
  • La qualité de l'air à Montréal (2023) — A plain-language public-health overview (PDF) produced jointly by Montréal's Direction régionale de santé publique (DRSP), the Ville de Montréal, and Québec's environment ministry. It walks through the main air pollutants, their effects on health, how air quality is monitored, and what drives Montréal's poor-air-quality and smog days — a local complement to the national report above. In French.
  • DRSP advisory on pollutants along highways — the Turcot interchange (2018) — A formal advisory (PDF) from Montréal's public-health directorate (DRSP) on air pollutants near highways, focused on the Turcot interchange. It reviews the health effects of traffic pollution, models how NO₂ disperses around the highway, and recommends ways to reduce the surrounding population's exposure — directly relevant to the near-road stations this site maps. In French.
  • Asthme infantile et polluants du trafic routier (2016) — A doctoral thesis (Université de Montréal) by Louis-François Tétreault — also a co-author of the DRSP Turcot advisory above — quantifying how long-term exposure to road-traffic pollutants — NO₂ in particular — relates to childhood asthma in Québec, using birth-cohort data. The most in-depth, research-level treatment of the traffic-and-health link in this list. In French, with an English abstract.

Data

  • RSQA — gaseous pollutants (Données Montréal) — The City of Montréal's open-data publication of the RSQA network's continuous hourly measurements of gaseous pollutants (NO, NO₂, O₃, SO₂, CO and others). These are the raw measurements this visualizer is built on; the dataset page links to the source CSV archives and the data licence.

Code

  • OpenMobilityData / AirQualityMTL on GitHub — The complete source code for this site: the Rust/WebAssembly front end and the scripts that fetch and preprocess the RSQA archive. It is public so the methodology can be scrutinized and others can learn from how it was built; it is not, however, run as a collaborative project, so issues and pull requests may go unanswered.
